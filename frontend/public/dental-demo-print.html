Demo Script

Phase 1: Uncover the Pain 2 min
"Dr. [Name], quick question — when you're chairside with a patient, who's answering your phones?"

[Let them answer]

"And what happens when your front desk is checking in a patient, handling insurance, AND the phone rings?"

[They'll admit calls get missed]

"Here's the thing — 80% of callers won't leave a voicemail. They just call the next dentist on Google. You never even know you lost them."

→ Pause. Let that sink in.

Phase 2: Quantify the Problem 2 min
"Let me show you something. [Open ROI Calculator] How many calls would you estimate go unanswered each week?"

→ Adjust slider to their number (usually 8-15)

"And what's a new patient worth to you over their lifetime?"

→ Adjust avg value slider ($285-400 typical)

"Look at this. You're potentially losing [$X] every single month. That's [$X x 12] per year walking out your door."
Point at the red "Without AI" card. Let the numbers do the talking.
Phase 3: Show the Solution 3 min
"What if you had a receptionist that never takes a lunch break, never calls in sick, and answers every single call in 2 rings — 24/7?"

→ Pull out your phone

"Let me show you exactly what your patients would experience."

→ Call demo number on speakerphone. Let them hear the AI.

"Notice how natural that sounded? It's having a real conversation and booking appointments directly into your calendar."
Phase 4: Stack the Value 2 min
"But here's what really sets us apart — after every appointment, the AI can automatically text your patients asking for a Google review."
"You said you have [X] reviews right now. What if you had 200? 500? That's the difference between page 1 and page 3 on Google."
"And one more thing — the AI detects personality types. If a patient is anxious, it slows down and reassures them. If they're direct, it gets to the point fast."
Phase 5: Close 1 min
"So here's the math: For $99 a month — less than one new patient — you recover potentially [$X] per month. That's a [X]x return."
"We can have you live by [day]. Your front desk keeps doing what they do best — the AI just catches everything they can't. Want to get started?"

→ Shut up. Wait for response.

If they hesitate: "What questions do you have?" — Don't offer discounts.

Sample AI Responses (Dental FAQs)

Q: Do you accept my insurance?

AI: "We work with most major dental insurance providers including Delta Dental, Cigna, MetLife, Aetna, and many others. I'd be happy to verify your specific coverage — can I get your insurance information and schedule a quick benefits check before your appointment?"

Q: How much does a cleaning cost?

AI: "A routine cleaning typically ranges from $95 to $150 depending on your specific needs. If you have insurance, most plans cover preventive cleanings at 100%. Would you like me to check your coverage and get you scheduled?"

Q: I have a toothache, can I come in today?

AI: "I'm sorry to hear you're in pain — we take dental emergencies seriously. Let me check our schedule for the earliest available time today. Can you tell me more about the pain?"

Q: Do you do teeth whitening?

AI: "Yes! We offer both in-office whitening that can brighten your smile up to 8 shades in about an hour, and take-home kits. Would you like to schedule a consultation?"

Q: I'm nervous about going to the dentist...

AI: "You're definitely not alone — dental anxiety is really common. We offer several comfort options including nitrous oxide and noise-canceling headphones. Would you like me to make a note so the team knows to take extra care with you?"

Q: Are you accepting new patients?

AI: "Absolutely! We'd love to welcome you to the practice. For new patients, we schedule a comprehensive exam and cleaning which takes about 90 minutes. Would you like me to find a time that works?"

Objection Handlers

"We already have a receptionist."

Response: "Absolutely — and she's probably great! This isn't about replacing her. It's about catching the calls she physically can't answer when she's checking someone in or at lunch. The AI handles overflow so she can focus on patients in front of her."

"Our patients want to talk to a real person."

Response: "I totally get that — and 70% of the time, they will. But when your real person can't answer, would patients rather talk to an AI that books them immediately... or get voicemail and call your competitor?"

"It's too expensive / I need to think about it."

Response: "$99 a month is basically one cleaning. If the AI books just ONE new patient per month you would've lost — it's paid for itself 3x over. The question isn't can you afford it, it's can you afford to keep losing [$X] per month?"

"What if it makes mistakes?"

Response: "Great question. The AI is trained specifically for dental practices. If it encounters something unusual, it says 'Let me have someone call you back' and flags it. You're always in control, and every call is recorded."

"I need to talk to my partner/office manager."

Response: "Of course. Let me send you the ROI calculator link so you can show them the numbers. And I can do a quick call with both of you later this week. What day works?"

"How long does setup take?"

Response: "24-48 hours. We need your hours, services, and insurance info — about 15 minutes of your time. Then we configure, test, and flip the switch. Your front desk doesn't need to learn anything new."
