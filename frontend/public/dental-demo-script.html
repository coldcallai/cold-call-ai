"We already have a receptionist."

Absolutely — and she's probably great! This isn't about replacing her. It's about catching the calls she physically can't answer when she's checking someone in, verifying insurance, or at lunch. The AI handles overflow so she can focus on the patients in front of her. No more "please hold" or missed calls.

"Our patients want to talk to a real person."

I totally get that — and 70% of the time, they will. But here's the thing: when your real person can't answer, would patients rather talk to an AI that sounds human and books them immediately... or get voicemail and call your competitor? We're not replacing human connection. We're catching the ones you'd otherwise lose.

Totally fair. Let's look at the math one more time: $99 a month is basically one cleaning.
